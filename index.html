Executive Summary

  • Liquidity squeeze deepens: Slovak firms faced a record-high credit line financing gap of +14.8pp—more than double the prior quarter—as demand surged while availability stagnated, signaling acute short-term funding stress.
  • Margin collapse accelerates: A net 40% of Slovak firms reported falling profits (up 17.4pp wave-over-wave) while labour costs rose for 84% and non-labour input prices were expected to climb 8.31%, creating a 4pp cost-revenue mismatch against selling price expectations of 4.27%.
  • Cost pressures diverge: Slovak firms’ upside inflation risk (net 59%) and non-labour input price expectations (8.31%) outpaced the euro area by 4pp and 2.47pp, respectively, despite easing turnover optimism (net 7% expecting increases)—the widest pessimism gap in three waves.
  • 🔍 Working capital dominance: 55.7% of Slovak firms prioritized financing for inventory and working capital—the highest share in the euro area—despite fixed investment expectations rising to a net 15%, underscoring a liquidity-driven financing tilt amid rising costs.

Financing Conditions

Slovak firms report sharpest interest rate tightening reversal in two waves

Changes in Terms and Conditions of Bank Financing (Q10)

  • A net 5% of Slovak firms (n=62) reported tighter interest rates on bank financing, rising sharply from a net 8% easing in the previous quarter — though still well below the EA's net 27% and DE's net 38% tightening, suggesting Slovak firms face relatively less pressure compared to peers.
  • Non-interest financing costs (charges, fees, commissions) tightened for a net 41% of Slovak firms (n=60), easing marginally by 1.8 pp from the prior quarter's net 42%, and remaining above the EA's net 37% but below DE's net 49%, putting continued cost pressure on Slovak firms.
  • Credit line and loan size restrictions tightened for a net 6% of Slovak firms (n=62), easing slightly by 0.9 pp compared with the previous quarter's net 7%, while the EA saw a more pronounced tightening of a net 9%, indicating Slovak firms face relatively less financing access constraints than the euro area.

* Only firms that have used or applied for this type of financing in the past are asked this question. A lower n relative to the total sample is by design and does not indicate a data quality issue — see ECB SAFE methodology for details.

Credit line stress surged for Slovak firms as need spiked while bank loans eased

Financing Need vs Availability Gap (Q5/Q9)

  • The Slovak bank loan gap remained negative at -4.0pp (n=254), meaning availability (+1.4pp) still outpaced need (-2.6pp), leaving Slovak firms less stressed than the EA's +1.7pp gap — though the gap narrowed from -7.6pp the prior quarter as need edged up by 3.6pp.
  • In sharp contrast, the credit line gap widened to +14.8pp (n=278) — 2.2pp above the EA's +12.6pp and far above last quarter's +4.8pp — driven almost entirely by a demand-side spike: need rose 9.2pp to +12.3pp while availability barely shifted from -1.7pp to -2.5pp, signalling heightened liquidity risk for firms relying on revolving credit.
  • Trade credit stress eased, with the Slovak gap narrowing from +9.8pp to +6.3pp (n=150), as financing need fell from +10.1pp to +7.1pp, leaving Slovak firms less stressed than the EA average of +8.2pp.

* Only firms that have used or applied for this type of financing in the past are asked this question. A lower n relative to the total sample is by design and does not indicate a data quality issue — see ECB SAFE methodology for details.

Slovak firms' bank loan availability expectations deteriorated sharply in wave 38

Expected Availability of External Financing (Q23)

  • A net 5% of Slovak firms (n=130) expected bank loan availability to deteriorate over the coming quarters, worsening by 3.2 pp from a net 2% in the prior wave — signalling a notable shift in sentiment.
  • Slovak firms' pessimism on bank loans now exceeds that of the euro area (net 4% expecting deterioration, n=2,948), suggesting local supply tightness that goes beyond the broader EA trend.
  • Trade credit expectations remain the sharpest concern for Slovakia, with a net 10% of firms (n=79) anticipating deterioration — well above the EA's net 1% — though this eased marginally by 1.5 pp from the previous wave's net 12%.

Slovak firms heavily favour working capital and innovation financing over fixed investment

Purpose of Financing (Q6A)

  • 55.7% of Slovak firms (n=229) used financing for inventory and working capital — far above the EA's 39.5% and stable compared with 55.8% in the previous quarter, making it the dominant financing purpose in Slovakia, which suggests liquidity constraints or operational expansion needs are prioritized.
  • 32.8% of Slovak firms (n=227) cited developing and launching new products or services, rising 2.9 pp from the previous quarter's 29.9% and well above the EA average of 20.2%, signalling a continued innovation-financing tilt.
  • Fixed investment financing fell to 34.2% of Slovak firms (n=229), easing 6.1 pp from the prior quarter's 40.3% and now below the EA's 36.8%, while hiring and training rose 4.2 pp to 31.1% — bucking the EA's 3.1 pp decline, which indicates a shift toward labour and skills investment over physical assets.

Bank willingness to lend eased slightly but remains positive for Slovak firms

Factors Affecting Access to External Financing (Q11)

  • A net 7.8% of Slovak firms (n=306) reported that banks' willingness to provide credit improved, easing 2.6 pp from a net 10.4% in the previous quarter — still above the EA's net 5.5% and contrasting with Germany's net 4.4% deterioration, suggesting relatively better access to bank financing for Slovak firms.
  • The general economic outlook deteriorated for a net 44.4% of Slovak firms (n=442), worsening 6.1 pp from a net 38.3% in the prior quarter and substantially exceeding the EA's net 26.1% deterioration, putting pressure on firms' revenue expectations and collateral values.
  • Access to public financial support deteriorated for a net 33.8% of Slovak firms (n=298), worsening sharply by 8.8 pp from a net 25.0% the previous quarter — more than double the EA's net 15.5% deterioration — signalling a tightening in non-bank financing buffers.

Economic Situation of Firms

Slovak firms' turnover fell sharply while profit declines deepened well beyond EA peers

Business Situation Indicators (Q2)

  • A net 26% of Slovak firms (n=229) reported falling turnover — asking whether sales rose, held, or fell — worsening by 11.6 pp from a net 15% decline in the previous quarter, far below the EA's marginal net 1% improvement, putting further strain on liquidity and investment capacity.
  • A net 40% of Slovak firms (n=229) reported declining profits, deteriorating by 17.4 pp from a net 22% decline in the prior quarter, more than double the EA's net 17% profit contraction, raising concerns over long-term sustainability.
  • Labour costs rose for a net 84% of Slovak firms (n=229), worsening by 21.8 pp versus the previous quarter and running 21 pp above the EA's already-elevated net 63%, underscoring severe margin pressure — squeezing profitability and limiting pricing flexibility.

Slovak firms' turnover optimism fell sharply while EA confidence strengthened

Expected Changes in Turnover and Investment (Q26)

  • A net 7% of Slovak firms (n=228) expected turnover to increase over the coming quarters, falling 14.2 pp from a net 21% in the previous wave, while the EA rose to a net 26% (n=5,266) — a divergence of nearly 19 pp, suggesting weakening domestic demand conditions.
  • Slovak fixed investment expectations bucked the regional trend, rising to a net 15% (n=228, up 3.9 pp vs the prior wave), outpacing the EA's net 9% (n=5,266), which itself eased 3.1 pp from the previous quarter — implying firms may be prioritizing domestic capacity despite broader regional caution.
  • The gap between Slovak and EA turnover outlooks widened markedly — Slovak firms were 19 pp less optimistic than their EA peers in wave 38, compared with only a 2 pp gap in the previous wave, signalling a diverging economic trajectory.

Slovak firms expect input prices to outpace selling prices by over 4 percentage points

Price, Wage and Employment Expectations (Q31/Q34)

  • Slovak firms (n=418) expected average selling prices to rise 4.27% over the next 12 months, easing slightly by 0.36 pp from 4.63% the previous quarter, yet still above the EA's 3.63% (n=9,968) — suggesting continued pressure on profit margins.
  • Slovak firms anticipated 8.31% non-labour input price growth (n=417) — more than double the EA's 5.84% — creating a 4 pp margin squeeze against selling price expectations, broadly unchanged from 8.37% the prior quarter.
  • Slovak firms anticipate 4.21% wage growth (n=441) alongside 8.31% input price rises, compressing margins further; wage expectations eased 1.28 pp from the previous quarter's 5.49%, but remain well above the EA's 2.80%.

Slovak firms see upside inflation risks above euro area average in wave 38

Inflation Risk Outlook (Q33)

  • A net 59% of Slovak firms (n=229) saw five-year inflation risks skewed to the upside, rising 5.8 pp from a net 53% in the previous wave, outpacing the euro area's net 55% (n=5,278) — implying heightened cost uncertainty for investment planning.
  • The euro area's upside inflation risk balance rose by a larger 9.9 pp wave-over-wave, narrowing — but not closing — the gap with Slovakia, which has persistently led the EA on this measure — suggesting Slovak firms may face prolonged cost pressures relative to peers.
  • Slovakia's elevated and sticky upside risk reading, above both the EA (net 55%) and Germany (net 51%), signals continued local price persistence and adds to the broader pressure on Slovak firms' cost outlook.

Access to finance becomes more pressing for Slovak firms while all other problems ease

Most Pressing Business Problems (Q0B)

  • Slovak firms rated access to finance at 3.91 out of 10 (n=223) in wave 38, rising 0.19 points from 3.72 in the prior quarter — the only problem to increase in pressingness — while the EA score eased from 4.14 to 3.99, highlighting a growing divergence in financing challenges.
  • Skilled staff remained the most pressing problem for Slovak firms, scoring 6.07 (n=228), though it eased 0.50 points from the prior quarter's 6.57, compared with a smaller EA easing of 0.13 points to 6.51, suggesting Slovak firms still face acute labor shortages despite some improvement.
  • Regulation rose to a pressingness score of 6.18 (n=225) among Slovak firms, up 0.12 points from wave 37's 6.06, making it the second-most pressing concern in Slovakia and slightly above the EA's 5.89, indicating regulatory burdens are intensifying relative to peers.
