Executive Summary

  • Credit line squeeze: Slovak firms reported a sharp widening in the credit line financing gap to +14.8pp—the highest in two waves—driven by surging demand (+12.3pp) and deteriorating availability (-2.5pp), leaving firms more stressed than the euro area average (+12.6pp).
  • Cost-profit divergence: Labour costs rose for a net 84% of Slovak firms, outpacing the euro area by 21pp, while turnover fell for 26%—a 27pp gap with the EA—compressing profit margins as a net 40% of firms reported declining profits.
  • Liquidity shift: Despite easing trade credit stress (gap narrowed to +6.3pp), Slovak firms increasingly relied on short-term financing for working capital (55.7% of firms), while fixed investment financing fell 6.1pp to 34.2%, below the euro area average for the first time in recent waves.
  • Public support crunch: Access to public financial support deteriorated notably for a net 33.8% of Slovak firms—8.8pp worse than the prior quarter and more than double the euro area’s 15.5%—signalling a structural vulnerability in non-bank financing buffers.

Credit line stress surpassed bank loan relief for Slovak firms in wave 38

Financing Need vs Availability Gap (Q5/Q9)

  • The Slovak credit line financing gap widened sharply to +14.8pp in wave 38 (n=278) — up from +4.8pp in the prior wave — driven by a rise in need to a net +12.3pp while availability deteriorated to -2.5pp, leaving Slovak firms more stressed than the EA (gap +12.6pp), reversing the previous wave's favourable position.
  • Trade credit stress eased for Slovak firms, with the gap narrowing to +6.3pp (n=150) from +9.8pp in wave 37, as need fell from a net +10.1pp to +7.1pp and availability edged up to +0.8pp, placing Slovakia 1.9pp below the EA gap of +8.2pp — suggesting reduced pressure on working capital financing.
  • Bank loan conditions remained the least stressed instrument for Slovak firms, with the gap at -4.0pp (n=254) — unchanged in its favourable direction from -7.6pp in wave 37 — as availability held at +1.4pp, leaving Slovakia 5.7pp below the EA gap of +1.7pp.

Slovak firms' bank loan availability expectations deteriorated, broadly tracking EA

Expected Availability of External Financing (Q23)

  • A net 5% of Slovak firms (n=130) expected bank loan availability to deteriorate over the next quarter, worsening by 3.2 pp from a net 2% in the prior wave — closely mirroring the EA-wide shift of 3.4 pp to a net 4%, which signals tightening credit conditions ahead.
  • Slovak firms' trade credit availability expectations remained notably more pessimistic than the euro area average, with a net 10% of firms (n=79) anticipating deterioration versus just a net 1% across the EA — a gap of 9.4 pp, suggesting persistent local trade credit tightness not reflected in EA-wide data.
  • For credit lines and overdrafts, a net 7% of Slovak firms (n=138) expected deterioration, easing marginally by 1.3 pp from the prior wave, while remaining 3.3 pp more pessimistic than the EA's net 4% — implying continued constrained liquidity buffers for firms.

Slovak firms prioritise working capital and human capital over fixed investment

Purpose of Financing (Q6A)

  • Inventory and working capital remained the dominant financing purpose for Slovak firms (n=229), cited by 55.7% — 16.2 pp above the euro area's 39.5%, with virtually no change (Δ −0.1 pp) from the prior quarter, underscoring their continued reliance on short-term liquidity.
  • Slovak firms increased their use of financing for hiring and training to 31.1% (Δ +4.2 pp, n=227), widening the gap with the euro area — where the share fell 3.1 pp to 24.3% — to 6.8 pp, signalling a strategic shift toward human capital development.
  • Fixed investment financing fell 6.1 pp to 34.2% (n=229), dropping below the euro area average of 36.8% for the first time in recent waves, as Slovak firms continued to redirect resources toward liquidity needs, compressing long-term growth investments.

Bank willingness to lend remains positive in Slovakia but eased marginally

Factors Affecting Access to External Financing (Q11)

  • A net 7.8% of Slovak firms (n=306) reported that banks' willingness to provide credit improved — the most credit-supply-relevant indicator — easing marginally by 2.6 pp from a net 10.4% in the prior quarter, yet remaining above the EA net of 5.5% and well above Germany's net -4.4%, suggesting a still-favourable but weakening credit environment.
  • Access to public financial support (incl. guarantees) deteriorated notably for a net 33.8% of Slovak firms (n=298), widening by 8.8 pp from a net 25.0% in the prior quarter and far exceeding the EA net of 15.5%, pointing to a disproportionate constraint on non-bank financing buffers in Slovakia.
  • The general economic outlook deteriorated for a net 44.4% of Slovak firms (n=442), worsening by 6.1 pp versus the prior quarter's net 38.3% and running 18.3 pp below the already negative EA net of 26.1%, representing a material macro-level drag on financing conditions.

Economic Situation of Firms

Slovak turnover deteriorated sharply as cost pressures far outpaced the euro area

Business Situation Indicators (Q2)

  • A net 26% of Slovak firms (n=229) reported falling turnover in wave 38, worsening by 11.6 pp from the previous quarter's net 15%, while the EA recorded a marginally positive net 1% — a divergence of over 27 pp, signalling deteriorating demand conditions for Slovak firms.
  • Labour cost pressures intensified markedly, with a net 84% of Slovak firms (n=229) reporting rising labour costs — up 21.8 pp from the prior quarter and outpacing the EA's net 63% by 21 pp — compressing profit margins, as a net 40% of Slovak firms reported falling profits, compared with a net 17% across the EA.
  • Other (non-labour) cost pressures also broadened, with a net 72% of Slovak firms (n=229) citing higher materials and energy costs, rising 14.4 pp from the prior quarter, broadly in line with the EA increase but from a higher base — squeezing operating cash flows for Slovak firms.

Slovak turnover optimism fell sharply, diverging from rising EA expectations

Expected Changes in Turnover and Investment (Q26)

  • A net 7% of Slovak firms (n=228) expected turnover to increase over the coming quarters, falling moderately by 14.2 pp from a net 21% in the previous wave — while the EA moved in the opposite direction, rising to a net 26% (n=5,266), widening the SK–EA gap to nearly 19 pp, suggesting potential competitiveness concerns for Slovak firms.
  • Slovak fixed investment expectations bucked the broad trend, rising moderately to a net 15% (n=228), up 3.9 pp from a net 11% in the prior wave, outpacing both the EA (net 9%) and Germany (net −2%), which both recorded declines, indicating stronger confidence in growth prospects among Slovak firms.
  • German turnover expectations edged up marginally to a net 19%, rising 2.3 pp from the prior wave, broadly tracking the EA improvement and contrasting with Slovakia's sharp reversal.

Slovak firms report strongest upside inflation risk balance, exceeding euro area

Inflation Risk Outlook (Q33)

  • A net 59% of Slovak firms (n=229) saw upside risks to five-year inflation, the highest net balance in the latest wave, marginally exceeding the EA's net 55% and rising 5.8 pp from the prior wave's net 53% — underscoring heightened local pricing power concerns.
  • The EA upside inflation risk balance also rose, increasing 9.9 pp to a net 55% (n=5,278) — a larger wave-over-wave gain than Slovakia's 5.8 pp, suggesting broader euro area inflation concern is closing the gap with Slovak levels.
  • Slovakia has consistently reported an above-EA upside inflation risk balance, reinforcing the pattern of local price persistence signalled in prior waves alongside elevated input price expectations.

Regulation and production costs emerge as Slovak firms' top concerns

Most Pressing Business Problems (Q0B)

  • Costs of production or labour ranked as the most pressing problem among the seven tested for Slovak firms (n=228), scoring 6.19/10 — a moderate level — easing marginally by 0.15 points from 6.34 in the prior wave, while regulation scored nearly as high at 6.18/10 (n=225), rising slightly by 0.12 points and exceeding the EA average of 5.89 — highlighting persistent cost pressures that could squeeze margins.
  • Availability of skilled staff or experienced managers fell the most among all problems, declining by 0.50 points to 6.07/10 (n=228), dropping from the top-ranked concern in Wave 37 to third place, though it remains at a moderate level of pressure — suggesting easing but still significant labour market tightness.
  • Access to finance, though still a low priority at 3.91/10 (n=223) — well below the threshold for moderate concern — was the only problem to increase in pressingness by 0.19 points versus the prior wave, a divergence from the EA (3.99/10) where it continued to ease — indicating potential tightening in financing conditions for Slovak firms.
