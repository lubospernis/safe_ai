Zhrnutie

  • 🔍 Adopcia AI v počiatočnej fáze: 46,5 % slovenských firiem uvádza veľmi zriedkavé alebo pilotné využívanie AI, čo je o 13,8 percentuálneho bodu viac ako v eurozóne (32,7 %), zatiaľ čo mierne využívanie dosahuje 17,1 % oproti 31,2 % v eurozóne – slovenské zapojenie sa sústreďuje skôr na najranejšiu fázu adopcie ako na rozsiahle nasadenie.
  • Periférna integrácia: Zlepšovanie procesov mimo hlavných oblastí je hlavným dôvodom využívania AI na Slovensku (47,9 % oproti 42,2 % v eurozóne), zatiaľ čo zlepšovanie procesov v hlavných oblastiach zaostáva za eurozónou (11,7 % oproti 21,3 %), čo poukazuje na periférnu a nie operatívnu integráciu.
  • Investičné plány pod úrovňou eurozóny: Slovenské firmy očakávajú, že v nasledujúcich 12 mesiacoch pridelia 5,5 % celkových investícií na AI, čo je menej ako 7,3 % v eurozóne a 8,4 % v Nemecku, čo je v súlade s ich profilom adopcie v skoršej fáze a väčšou skepsou voči užitočnosti AI (31,5 % oproti 26,1 % v eurozóne).
  • Nafukovanie zahraničného benchmarku: Slovenské firmy odhadujú, že 25,6 % ich konkurentov v Nemecku, Francúzsku a Taliansku investovalo do AI do polovice roka 2025, oproti priemeru eurozóny 19,0 %, čo naznačuje optimistickejší pohľad na prenikanie AI v západných ekonomikách, aj keď očakávajú len málo od domácich konkurentov.

Špeciálna téma: Artificial intelligence technologies

Slovak firms trail the euro area in AI intensity and planned investment, yet overestimate peer adoption in larger economies.

Slovakia reports a higher share of firms in very infrequent/pilot use (46.5% vs 32.7%) and a lower share in moderate use (17.1% vs 31.2%) of AI technologies compared to the euro area.

QA1 — How would you assess the use of AI technologies in your firm?

  • 46.5% of Slovak firms report very infrequent or pilot use of AI, 13.8 pp above the EA's 32.7%, while moderate use stands at 17.1% versus 31.2% in the EA.
  • The gap in moderate use (17.1% SK vs 31.2% EA) points to a concentration of Slovak AI engagement at the earliest adoption stage rather than scaled deployment.

Slovakia’s top AI use reason is improving non-core processes (47.9%), slightly above the euro area’s 42.2%, while Slovak firms are less likely than the euro area to use AI for improving core processes (11.7% vs 21.3%).

QA2 — Please indicate the two main reasons your firm is using AI technologies.

  • Slovak AI-using firms are less likely than EA peers to cite improving core area processes as a primary reason (11.7% vs 21.3%), consistent with their lower rate of moderate or intensive AI use.
  • Improving non-core area processes is the top reason in Slovakia (47.9% vs EA 42.2%), reinforcing the pattern of peripheral rather than operational AI integration.

Slovak firms most often cite incompatibility with existing systems (28.4%) as the main reason for not using AI more intensively, while lack of AI skills is the top reason in the euro area (31.8%).

QA3 — Please indicate the two main reasons for [IF QA1_2025Q4 =1 “not using AI”] [IF QA1_2025Q4=2,3 “not using AI more intensively”] in your firm.

  • Incompatibility with existing systems is the leading barrier to greater AI use in Slovakia (28.4%), versus lack of AI skills in the euro area (31.8%); the gap on incompatibility between SK and EA is narrow (28.4% vs 30.1%).
  • Slovak firms cite 'AI is not useful for my firm' at 31.5% versus EA's 26.1%, the largest cross-country divergence among the barriers shown, reflecting lower perceived relevance.

Slovak firms expect to allocate 7.2% of their total investment to AI technologies over the next 12 months, compared to 9.1% in the Euro Area.

QA4 — Over the next 12 months, what percentage of your firm’s total investment do you expect to allocate to AI technologies?

  • Slovak firms expect to allocate 5.5% of total investment to AI over the next 12 months, below the EA's 7.3% and Germany's 8.4%, consistent with their earlier-stage adoption profile.

Slovak firms estimated 15.6% of similar firms in Slovakia had invested in AI by June 2025, slightly below the EA average of 17.1%, while Slovak firms estimated 27.2% of similar firms in Germany, France and Italy had invested, notably higher than the EA average of 20.8%.

QB1 — Thinking about other firms like yours, in the same sector and of a similar size, what percentage of these do you think had invested in AI technologies up to June 2025?

  • Slovak firms estimate that 25.6% of similar firms in Germany, France and Italy had invested in AI by June 2025, versus an EA average estimate of 19.0% — a 6.6 pp gap that implies Slovak firms hold a more optimistic view of western-economy AI penetration than EA respondents overall.
  • For domestic peers, Slovak firms estimate 14.1% had invested in AI by mid-2025, 1.5 pp below the EA average of 15.6%, broadly in line with the EA.

Slovak firms expect 25.5% of domestic peers to invest in AI over the next 12 months, 6.4pp below the EA’s 31.9%, while their outlook for Germany, France and Italy (32.8%) is close to the EA’s 34.9%.

QB2 — Thinking about the next 12 months, what percentage of firms similar to yours do you expect to make investments in AI technologies?

  • Slovak firms expect only 23.7% of domestic SME peers to invest in AI over the next 12 months, 6.8 pp below the EA's 30.5%, while expectations for large domestic peers (23.4% vs EA 26.1%) show a smaller gap.
  • For peers in Germany, France and Italy, Slovak firms' expectations are close to or above the EA average — 31.4% vs EA 33.3% for SMEs and 35.0% vs EA 29.4% for large firms — continuing the pattern of more optimistic assessments of western-economy AI investment.
