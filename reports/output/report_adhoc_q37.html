Executive Summary

  • 🔍 AI adoption lags frontier: Slovak firms remain concentrated at pilot-stage AI use (46.5% very infrequent or pilot versus 32.7% in the EA, a 13.8 pp gap), while moderate use is substantially less prevalent (17.1% versus 31.2%).
  • Non-core use case: Slovak AI-using firms prioritise improving non-core processes (47.9%) over core operations, a 25.8 pp divergence from the EA (21.3%) — the largest cross-country gap across AI use reasons and consistent with lower investment intensity.
  • Under-committing at home: Slovak firms plan to allocate 5.5% of total investment to AI over the next 12 months, 1.8 pp below the EA mean of 7.3%, even as they perceive large-economy peers to be adopting faster than the EA average (25.6% versus 19.0%).

Special Focus: Artificial intelligence technologies

Slovak firms remain concentrated at pilot-stage AI use and allocate less investment to AI than the EA average.

Slovakia has a higher share of firms using AI very infrequently or experimentally (SK 46.5% vs EA 32.7% on code 2) and a lower share with moderate use (SK 17.1% vs EA 31.2% on code 3).

QA1 — How would you assess the use of AI technologies in your firm?

  • Slovak firms are notably more concentrated at the pilot/experimental stage than EA peers: 46.5% of SK firms report very infrequent or pilot use versus 32.7% in the EA, a gap of 13.8 pp.
  • Moderate AI use is substantially less prevalent in Slovakia (17.1%) than in the EA (31.2%), a difference of 14.1 pp, with significant use also below EA levels.

In Slovakia, the top AI use reason is improving non-core processes (47.9%), while in the EA it is improving core processes (37.6%).

QA2 — Please indicate the two main reasons your firm is using AI technologies.

  • Slovak AI-using firms prioritise improving non-core area processes (47.9%) as their primary reason, while EA counterparts most frequently cite improving core area processes (37.6%), with SK at only 12.0% on that code.
  • The 25.8 pp gap between SK (47.9%) and EA (21.3%) on improving non-core processes is the largest cross-country divergence observed across AI use reasons.

In Slovakia, the top reason for not using AI more intensively is incompatibility with existing systems (SK 28.4%), while in the EA the top reason is lack of AI skills (EA 31.8%).

QA3 — Please indicate the two main reasons for [IF QA1_2025Q4 =1 “not using AI”] [IF QA1_2025Q4=2,3 “not using AI more intensively”] in your firm.

  • Incompatibility with existing systems is the leading barrier in Slovakia (28.4%), whereas lack of AI skills tops the EA list (31.8%), pointing to differing structural constraints across the two geographies.
  • Slovakia's rate on the incompatibility barrier (28.4%) is broadly comparable to the EA (30.1%), a gap of only 1.7 pp, while the skills deficit barrier is more pronounced in the EA.

Slovak firms expect to allocate 7.2% of total investment to AI technologies over the next 12 months, compared to 9.1% in the Euro Area.

QA4 — Over the next 12 months, what percentage of your firm’s total investment do you expect to allocate to AI technologies?

  • Slovak firms expect to allocate 5.5% of total investment to AI over the next 12 months, 1.8 pp below the EA mean of 7.3% and 2.9 pp below Germany's 8.4%, the lowest of the three shown.

Slovak firms estimated 15.6% of similar domestic firms had invested in AI by June 2025, slightly below the EA’s 17.1% (code a), while they estimated 27.2% for Germany, France and Italy versus the EA’s 20.8% (code b).

QB1 — Thinking about other firms like yours, in the same sector and of a similar size, what percentage of these do you think had invested in AI technologies up to June 2025?

  • Slovak firms perceive AI adoption among large-economy peers (Germany, France and Italy) to be notably higher than the EA average: SK respondents estimate 25.6% of such peers have invested in AI versus the EA estimate of 19.0%, a gap of 6.6 pp.
  • For domestic peer adoption, Slovak firms estimate 14.1% had invested by mid-2025, slightly below the EA mean of 15.6% (1.5 pp gap).

For investments in AI technologies in the next 12 months, Slovakia’s firms expect 25.5% of domestic peers to invest (SK a_g3) versus 31.9% in the EA (EA a_g3), while they expect 36.4% of firms in Germany, France and Italy to invest (SK b_g4) versus 31.1% in the EA (EA b_g4).

QB2 — Thinking about the next 12 months, what percentage of firms similar to yours do you expect to make investments in AI technologies?

  • Slovak firms expect 35.0% of large-firm peers in Germany, France and Italy to invest in AI over the next 12 months, 5.6 pp above the EA expectation of 29.4%, while expectations for domestic large-firm peers (23.4%) trail the EA (26.1%) by 2.7 pp.
  • For domestic SME peers, Slovak firms expect 23.7% to invest, compared with 30.5% in the EA, a gap of 6.8 pp, the widest domestic-peer divergence across the QB2 sub-items.
