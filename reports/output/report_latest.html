Executive Summary

The most consequential finding for Slovakia in Wave 38 is a sharp deterioration in the business situation, with a net 31% of firms reporting falling turnover—markedly worse than the euro area's net −7%—and profit declines reaching a net −42% versus −24% for the EA, the steepest recorded among reported economies. Labour cost pressures intensified simultaneously, with a net 85% of firms reporting increases against an EA net of 61%. On financing conditions, non-interest costs remained severely elevated at a net 40.6%, modestly above the EA (net 36.6%), while interest rate tightening (net 5.5%) lagged well behind EA peers (net 27.3%). The credit line financing gap widened adversely to +14.8pp, exceeding the EA's +12.6pp. The economic outlook deteriorated sharply, with a net 44% of Slovak firms reporting worsening conditions versus the EA's net −27%, underscoring mounting macro headwinds.

  • A net 5.5% of Slovak firms (n=62) reported an increase in interest rates, a shift from net easing of -8.3% in the previous wave, though conditions remain considerably less tight than in the EA (net 27.3%, n=1,892) and DE (net 37.5%).

Most Pressing Business Problems (Q0B)

  • Slovak firms rated access to finance as moderately pressing (3.87 out of 10, n=199), virtually in line with the EA average (3.97), and easing slightly from 4.04 in the previous wave.
  • Access to finance remained the least pressing problem for Slovak firms in Wave 38, well below the most pressing concern — costs of production or labour (5.91) and availability of skilled staff (5.90).
  • Slovak firms consistently rate all business obstacles below EA averages, with the access-to-finance gap of 0.10 points versus the EA being among the smallest divergences across all problem categories.

Business Situation Indicators (Q2)

  • A net 31% of Slovak firms (n=205) reported a decline in turnover in Wave 38, deteriorating from a net −22% in the previous wave and markedly underperforming the EA average of −7%.
  • Slovak labour costs pressures intensified sharply, with a net 85% of firms (n=205) reporting increases—up from a net 66% in Wave 37 and well above the EA net of 61%—compounding an already adverse cost environment.
  • Slovak firms recorded the steepest profit decline among the reported economies, at a net −42% (n=205) versus a net −24% for the EA, worsening from a net −32% in the prior wave.

Financing Need vs Availability Gap (Q5/Q9)

  • Slovak firms reported a negative bank loan financing gap of -4.0pp (n=242), meaning availability exceeded need, contrasting with the EA's adverse gap of +1.7pp (n=5,650), though the Slovak gap improved from -7.6pp in the previous wave.
  • For credit lines and overdrafts, Slovak firms faced an adverse financing gap of +14.8pp (n=262), widening sharply from +4.8pp in the prior wave and exceeding the EA gap of +12.6pp (n=4,904).
  • The Slovak trade credit financing gap eased to +6.3pp (n=150) from +9.8pp previously, remaining below the EA gap of +8.2pp (n=3,768), suggesting relatively less pressure on trade credit access.

Purpose of Financing (Q6A)

  • Slovak firms (n=229) cited inventory and working capital most frequently (55.7%), markedly above the EA average (39.5%, n=5,256) and broadly unchanged from the previous wave (55.8%).
  • 32.8% of Slovak firms cited developing and launching new products or services, well above the EA share (20.2%, n=5,254) and up from 29.9% in the prior wave, suggesting a stronger innovation-driven financing motive.
  • Fixed investment was cited by only 34.2% of Slovak firms (n=229), below both the EA average (36.8%) and the previous wave (40.3%), pointing to a relative shift away from capital expenditure financing.

Factors Affecting Access to External Financing (Q11)

  • A net 6% of Slovak firms (n=270) reported improved bank willingness to provide credit, broadly in line with the EA (net +6%), though easing slightly from a net 8% in the previous wave.
  • The general economic outlook deteriorated further for Slovak firms (n=398), with a net 44% reporting worsening conditions, compared with a net 38% in the previous wave and a steeper decline than the EA average of net −27%.
  • Data on access to public financial support were unavailable for Slovakia in Wave 38, precluding comparison with the EA's net −17% (n=6,472), which itself improved from net −19% in Wave 37.

Bank Loan Access: Applications and Financing Gap (Q7A/Q7B)

  • Slovak financing gap data are unavailable for Wave 38 (n=130), preventing direct comparison with the EA figure of 6.44% of firms (n=3,421).
  • In the previous wave, 4.97% of Slovak firms (n=130) faced financing constraints, below the EA average of 5.78%, suggesting relatively easier bank loan access.
  • The EA financing gap widened from 5.78% to 6.44% of firms in Wave 38, signalling a broad deterioration in access to bank loans across the euro area.
