Executive Summary

The most striking finding for Slovakia in Wave 38 is the sharp deterioration in business fundamentals, with a net 31% of firms reporting falling turnover — markedly worse than the euro area average of net –7% — compounding a net 42% reporting declining profits against a net 24% EA-wide. Labour costs intensified this squeeze, with a net 85% of Slovak firms recording increases versus a net 61% across the euro area. Despite this, financing conditions remain comparatively supportive: Slovakia's bank loan financing gap of –4.0pp indicates availability continues to outpace need, contrasting with the EA's adverse gap of +1.7pp. Non-interest financing costs remain a persistent burden, with a net 40.6% reporting increases, above the EA net of 36.6%. The macro outlook is the sharpest warning signal, with a net 44% of Slovak firms citing deteriorating economic conditions, well exceeding the EA net of –27%.

Changes in Terms and Conditions of Bank Financing (Q10)

  • A net 5.5% of Slovak firms (n=62) reported an increase in interest rates, a notable shift from net easing of -8.3% in the previous wave, though remaining well below the EA (net 27.3%, n=1,892) and DE (net 37.5%).
  • A net 40.6% of Slovak firms (n=60) reported higher non-interest financing costs, broadly in line with DE (net 48.7%) and above the EA average (net 36.6%, n=1,902), with little change from net 42.4% in the prior wave.
  • Loan size availability tightened for a net 6.1% of Slovak firms (n=62), marginally easing from net 7.0% previously, while collateral requirements (net 1.4%) and other terms (net 8.0%) both showed modest tightening after reporting no net balance in wave 37.

* Only firms that have used or applied for this type of financing in the past are asked this question. A lower n relative to the total sample is by design and does not indicate a data quality issue — see ECB SAFE methodology for details.

Most Pressing Business Problems (Q0B)

  • In Wave 38, Slovak firms rated access to finance at 3.87 (n=199), nearly in line with the EA average of 3.97, and little changed from 4.04 in Wave 37.
  • Access to finance remains the least pressing concern for Slovak firms, well below the most pressing problem, finding customers, scored at 5.09 (n=203) in Wave 38.
  • Slovak firms consistently rate all problems below EA averages, except regulation (5.92 vs. 5.76 EA) and "other" (5.38 vs. 4.36 EA, n=180), suggesting idiosyncratic cost pressures beyond standard categories.

Business Situation Indicators (Q2)

  • A net 31% of Slovak firms (n=205) reported a decline in turnover, deteriorating from a net 22% in the previous wave and markedly worse than the EA average of –7%.
  • Slovak firms reported sharply rising labour costs, with a net 85% (n=205) recording increases, exceeding the EA net of 61% and up from a net 66% in the prior wave.
  • Profitability deteriorated further, with a net 42% of Slovak firms (n=205) reporting falling profits, compared with a net 32% in the previous quarter and a net 24% across the EA.

Financing Need vs Availability Gap (Q5/Q9)

  • For bank loans in Slovakia (n=254), the financing gap improved to –4.0pp from –7.6pp in the prior wave, as availability held steady at a net +1.4pp while need shifted less negatively (–2.6pp vs. –6.2pp), narrowing the surplus of availability over need.
  • Slovakia's bank loan gap of –4.0pp contrasts sharply with the EA gap of +1.7pp (n=5,784), indicating Slovak firms continued to enjoy relatively favourable access conditions where availability outpaced need, unlike the EA aggregate.

* Only firms that have used or applied for this type of financing in the past are asked this question. A lower n relative to the total sample is by design and does not indicate a data quality issue — see ECB SAFE methodology for details.

Purpose of Financing (Q6A)

  • Slovak firms (n=229) cited inventory and working capital as a financing purpose far more frequently (55.7%) than the EA average (39.5%), a gap that has persisted from the previous wave (55.8% vs. 38.8%).
  • The share of Slovak firms citing new product development rose to 32.8% in Wave 38 (from 29.9%), notably exceeding the EA average of 20.2% (n=5,254) and suggesting a stronger innovation-oriented financing motive.
  • Fixed investment was cited by 34.2% of Slovak firms (n=229), down from 40.3% in the prior wave and now slightly below the EA average of 36.8% (n=5,263), indicating a relative pullback in capex-driven financing needs.

Factors Affecting Access to External Financing (Q11)

  • A net 6% of Slovak firms (n=270) reported improved bank willingness to provide credit, broadly in line with the EA net of +6%, though easing slightly from a net +8% in the previous wave.
  • Slovak firms reported a sharply deteriorating general economic outlook, with a net 44% (n=398) citing worsening conditions, compared with a net 38% in the prior wave and the EA net of −27%, signalling a pronounced macro-level drag on financing conditions.
  • Data on access to public financial support were unavailable for Slovakia in Wave 38 (n=258), limiting comparison with the EA net of −17%, which itself improved from −19% the previous wave.
