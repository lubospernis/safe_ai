Executive Summary

  • Non-price cost surge: Non-interest borrowing costs tightened for a net 42% of Slovak firms, far outpacing the EA’s 28% and offsetting lower interest rates, intensifying the liquidity squeeze.
  • Margin collapse: A net 62% of Slovak firms saw labour costs rise while a net 15% reported falling turnover, driving a net 22% profit decline—both metrics worsened more than in the EA.
  • Credit access eases, investment stalls: The bank loan financing gap narrowed sharply to +7.6pp from +10.5pp, yet fixed investment expectations plunged to a net 11%, 14.9pp below the prior wave.
  • 🔍 AI adoption lag: Slovak firms trail the EA in AI uptake (15.6% vs 17.1% current adoption), with 31.7% dismissing AI as irrelevant, risking productivity divergence despite similar investment shares.

Financing Conditions

Slovak firms report easing interest rates even as non-interest charges tighten sharply

Changes in Terms and Conditions of Bank Financing (Q10)

  • A net 8% of Slovak firms (n=48) reported an easing in the level of interest rates on bank loans — a marked reversal, rising 16.9 pp from a net 25% easing in the previous quarter, yet still favourably diverging from the EA's net 11% tightening and Germany's net 28% tightening, suggesting improved debt servicing capacity for borrowers.
  • Non-interest financing costs (charges, fees, commissions) tightened for a net 42% of Slovak firms (n=48), far above the EA's net 28% and second only to Germany's net 48%, though no prior-wave SK figure is available for direct wave-on-wave comparison — putting upward pressure on overall borrowing costs despite lower interest rates.
  • The available size of loan or credit lines tightened for a net 7% of Slovak firms (n=48), swinging 14.9 pp from a net 8% easing in the previous quarter, and sitting above both the EA (net 4% tightening) and Germany (net 2% tightening) — limiting firms' access to liquidity despite cheaper borrowing.

* Only firms that have used or applied for this type of financing in the past are asked this question. A lower n relative to the total sample is by design and does not indicate a data quality issue — see ECB SAFE methodology for details.

Slovak bank loan stress reversed sharply while trade credit gap remained elevated

Financing Need vs Availability Gap (Q5/Q9)

  • For bank loans, a net 7.6% more Slovak firms (n=240) reported availability exceeding need — a dramatic reversal from a net stress gap of +10.5pp the previous quarter — driven by need falling to -6.2pp while availability turned positive at +1.4pp, leaving Slovakia markedly less stressed than the EA's +3.7pp gap.
  • The credit line gap narrowed to +4.8pp (need: +3.1pp, avail: -1.7pp, n=256), easing 8.3pp from the previous quarter's +13.1pp and falling below the EA's +6.6pp gap, signalling that the acute credit line stress of the prior wave has largely unwound for Slovak firms.
  • Trade credit remained the most stressed instrument for Slovakia, with a gap of +9.8pp (n=176) — above the EA's +6.8pp — as need rose to +10.1pp while availability was near flat at +0.3pp, though this still represents an improvement from the prior quarter's +16.4pp gap, underscoring persistent liquidity pressures for firms relying on this instrument.

* Only firms that have used or applied for this type of financing in the past are asked this question. A lower n relative to the total sample is by design and does not indicate a data quality issue — see ECB SAFE methodology for details.

Slovak firms' credit access gap nearly halved, bucking the EA trend

Bank Loan Applications and Access Conditions (Q7A/Q7B)

  • The share of Slovak firms seeking bank loans that either feared rejection or were turned down (the financing gap) fell from 7.38% (wave 36, n=90) to 3.58% (wave 37, n=147) — easing 3.80 pp — while the EA's equivalent rate rose from 3.54% to 5.02% (n=3,406), meaning Slovakia moved from above to well below the EA, signalling improved credit access conditions.
  • Slovak firms' application rate (share that actually applied for a bank loan) fell from 46.16% to 23.51% (n=147), compared with the EA where it rose from 30.55% to 35.93% (n=3,406); the rejection rate among Slovak applicants also eased, falling from 8.78% to 5.31%, while the EA rejection rate rose from 2.73% to 5.00% — suggesting a potential shift in firms' borrowing strategies or credit conditions.
  • Slovak firms' discouragement rate (share that did not apply out of fear of rejection) edged down from 3.33% to 2.34% (n=147), marginally below the EA's 3.22% (n=3,406), suggesting reduced — not heightened — credit-access anxiety among Slovak firms in the latest quarter.

Slovak firms' bank loan availability expectations recover sharply from prior-quarter trough

Expected Availability of External Financing (Q23)

  • A net 2% of Slovak firms (n=125) expected bank loan availability to deteriorate over the coming quarters, improving markedly by 10.7 pp from the previous quarter's net 13% — the sharpest single-quarter recovery in recent waves, suggesting firms may now face fewer financing constraints.
  • Slovak and EA firms now hold closely aligned — and mildly pessimistic — bank loan outlooks (net 2% and net 0.5% expecting deterioration, respectively), whereas in the prior quarter Slovak firms were far more pessimistic than EA peers (net 13% vs net 1% expecting deterioration), indicating a convergence in financing sentiment across regions.
  • Trade credit expectations remained notably weaker for Slovak firms, with a net 12% (n=89) expecting deterioration — more than 20 pp below the EA's near-neutral reading — signalling that local supply-chain financing stress persists even as the bank loan outlook has normalised, which could still hinder SMEs reliant on supplier credit.

Slovak firms increasingly used financing for working capital and innovation

Purpose of Financing (Q6A)

  • 55.8% of Slovak firms (n=237) cited inventory and working capital as a financing purpose, up from 53.2% the prior quarter and 17 pp above the EA's 38.8% — highlighting a persistent liquidity need that may strain cash flow management.
  • 29.9% of Slovak firms (n=237) used financing to develop or launch new products or services, rising 8.4 pp from the previous quarter and outpacing the EA's 21.8% — suggesting stronger investment in growth despite higher financing costs.
  • 20.6% of Slovak firms (n=236) cited refinancing or paying off obligations, up 5.9 pp from 14.7% the prior quarter, now above the EA's 14.3% but still below Germany's 22.2% — indicating elevated debt servicing pressure relative to peers.

Bank willingness to lend to Slovak firms improved sharply in wave 37

Factors Affecting Access to External Financing (Q11)

  • A net 10% of Slovak firms (n=300) reported that banks' willingness to provide credit improved, reversing from a net 2% deterioration in the prior wave — and now exceeding the EA's net 6% improvement, suggesting stronger credit supply conditions for firms.
  • The general economic outlook remained a macro drag, with a net 38% of Slovak firms (n=460) reporting deterioration, though this eased by 12.9 pp compared with the previous quarter's net 51% deterioration, still weighing on financing prospects.
  • Access to public financial support continued to deteriorate for a net 25% of Slovak firms (n=328), improving 8.9 pp from the prior wave's net 34%, yet still markedly worse than the EA's net 15% deterioration — limiting alternative financing options for firms.

Economic Situation of Firms

Slovak firms report falling turnover and rising costs, squeezing profits

Business Situation Indicators (Q2)

  • A net 15% of Slovak firms (n=237) reported declining turnover — where more firms saw revenues fall than rise — broadly unchanged from a net 15% in the previous quarter, contrasting with a net 6% improvement across the EA, suggesting sustained revenue headwinds for firms.
  • Slovak firms faced the steepest cost pressures in the region: a net 62% reported rising labour costs (n=237), up 8 pp from the prior quarter's net 54%, exceeding both the EA (net 50%) and DE (net 55%), putting significant pressure on margins.
  • A net 22% of Slovak firms (n=237) reported falling profits, improving by 15 pp from the prior quarter's net 38%, yet remaining worse than the EA's net 10% decline, reflecting ongoing margin compression.

Slovak firms' investment outlook fell sharply while turnover optimism eased

Expected Changes in Turnover and Investment (Q26)

  • A net 21% of Slovak firms (n=169) expected turnover to increase over the next two quarters, easing 3.3 pp from a net 24% in the prior wave — remaining broadly in line with the EA's net 23%.
  • Slovak firms' fixed investment expectations fell markedly to a net 11% (n=169), down 14.9 pp from a net 26% in the previous wave, widening the gap versus the EA's more stable net 13%.
  • The sharp drop in Slovak investment expectations — compared with a much smaller 1.3 pp easing for EA firms — signals a diverging capital spending trajectory that may weigh on Slovak growth prospects.

Slovak firms expect selling prices to rise 4.6%, far above EA average

Price, Wage and Employment Expectations (Q31/Q34)

  • A net 4.63% average selling price increase was expected by Slovak firms (n=226), rising 1.22 pp from 3.41% in the previous quarter and well above the EA's 2.77%, signalling persistent price pressures.
  • Slovak firms anticipate 8.37% input price rises (n=230) — more than double the EA's 3.53% — alongside 5.49% wage growth (n=233), compressing margins as cost expectations accelerated 2.52 pp and 0.11 pp respectively versus the prior quarter.
  • Slovak firms' 12-month euro area inflation expectations rose to 4.79% (n=211), up 0.45 pp from the previous quarter's 4.34%, diverging further from the EA-wide average of 3.36%, which eased 0.04 pp over the same period.

Slovak firms see upside inflation risks at highest rate among peers

Inflation Risk Outlook (Q33)

  • A net 53% of Slovak firms (n=237) saw upside risks to five-year inflation — meaning more firms expected inflation to be higher than expected than lower — compared with a net 45% across the EA (n=5,067), signalling persistent local price pressures.
  • Slovak firms' upside inflation risk balance eased by 7 pp from a net 60% in the previous wave (n=134), yet remained the highest among the three economies shown, sitting 8 pp above the EA and 12 pp above Germany's net 41%.
  • Germany recorded the sharpest wave-over-wave rise, with its net upside risk balance climbing 21 pp from the prior quarter, narrowing but not closing the gap with Slovakia's still-elevated reading — highlighting divergent inflation expectations across the region.

Skilled staff shortage remains Slovak firms' most pressing business problem

Most Pressing Business Problems (Q0B)

  • Slovak firms (n=237) rated availability of skilled staff as their most pressing problem at 6.57 out of 10, broadly in line with the EA average of 6.64 (n=5,033) and up 0.12 points since wave 30 — highlighting persistent operational bottlenecks for growth.
  • Access to finance remained the least pressing concern for Slovak firms (n=233), scoring 3.72 out of 10 — below both the EA (4.14, n=4,985) and DE (4.33) averages, and virtually unchanged (Δ −0.01) since wave 30, suggesting limited immediate financial distress.
  • Regulation emerged as the fastest-rising pressure for Slovak firms (n=233), with its average pressingness score rising by 1.06 points since wave 30 to reach 6.06 — the largest increase of any problem category and now matching the EA average of 6.04, indicating growing compliance challenges.

Special Focus: AI Adoption

AI Use in Own Firm

Slovak firms plan AI investment share equal to the Euro Area median.

  • **AI use intensity:** 46.5% of Slovak firms report infrequent/pilot use vs 32.7% in the Euro Area.
  • **Investment focus:** Both Slovak and Euro Area firms allocate a median 5% of total investment to AI next year.

Why Firms Use or Avoid AI

Slovak AI-using firms prioritize non-core process improvement over core, unlike the EA.

  • **Top AI driver:** 47.9% of Slovak firms (vs 42.2% EA) cite improving non-core processes as their primary AI use.
  • **AI barrier:** 31.7% of Slovak firms (vs 26.1% EA) see AI as not useful for their business as the main barrier.

Peer AI Adoption Expectations

Slovak firms expect slower peer AI adoption than Euro Area peers.

  • **Current adoption:** Slovak firms estimate 15.6% of local peers have invested in AI today, below the Euro Area’s 17.1%.
  • **Next 12 months:** Slovak SMEs expect 25.5% adoption locally, versus 31.9% in the Euro Area.
